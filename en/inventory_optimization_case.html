Case Study

Inventory Optimization & Decision Support

A practical optimization project combining applied mathematics, operations research, and Python-based analytics to improve inventory policy, warehouse efficiency, and decision quality in an industrial environment.

Back to Portfolio View Results

Overview

This case illustrates how mathematical modeling can be translated into operational improvements in production and supply chain environments. The project focused on inventory policy design, replenishment logic, warehouse footprint optimization, and scenario-based decision support.

The objective was not only to reduce cost, but also to create a more robust decision framework for managing uncertainty, service levels, lead times, and storage constraints across a product portfolio.

Business Challenge

The operating environment required a better balance between shortage risk, service-level targets, and holding costs. Existing replenishment practices needed a more structured analytical basis that could account for lead times, minimum order quantities, warehouse limitations, and heterogeneous demand patterns.

A second challenge was workflow efficiency. Sequential and parallel production processes affected order completion time, while warehouse allocation decisions influenced both cost and operational flexibility.

Analytical Approach

The project used tools from applied mathematics and operations research to convert practical business constraints into optimization logic. The analysis included demand segmentation, safety stock estimation, target inventory calculation, replenishment rules, and cost-risk trade-off assessment.

  • Demand segmentation by regular, irregular, and seasonal consumption patterns.
  • Inventory policy design using service level, lead time, MOQ, and capacity constraints.
  • Safety stock and target stock calculations for more reliable replenishment decisions.
  • Scenario analysis and sensitivity testing for decision robustness.
  • Production workflow optimization for reduced cycle time.
  • Warehouse consolidation logic for storage cost reduction.

Solution Design

A Python-based decision-support tool was developed to help evaluate alternative replenishment settings and compare operational scenarios. The tool enabled structured analysis of trade-offs between availability, working capital, and storage efficiency.

In parallel, optimization models were applied to production sequencing and warehouse placement logic. This made it possible to improve operational throughput while reducing excess storage usage.

Why It Matters

This project demonstrates my approach to industrial analytics: start from a real operational problem, formalize it mathematically, and deliver a solution that is both technically rigorous and practically usable by decision-makers.

It also reflects the broader focus of my work at the intersection of applied mathematics, optimization, data science, and decision support for complex technical environments.
